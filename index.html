The Mystery Microbe

This website covers 4 experiments conducted on a microbe taken from a succulent plant. These experiments were done with the goal of learning more about this microbe and how it interacts with the world. The microbe has a dark green, fuzzy appearance, and grows in many circular formations all over the plate.

Conclusion

We have learned many things from these four experiments. The main takeaways are that the microbe is most likely found in areas with warmer climates or during warmer seasons, the microbe best grows with access to nutrients, such as on a tomato plant, and that the microbe must compete with other microbes for resources. I would like to know how common this microbe is on plants like mine, and what areas around the world it's most commonly found in. I don't think these experiments gave enough information to identify the species of the microbe. If I were to try to identify the species, I would continue conduct more experiments, starting with the many ideas mentioned in the experiment pages, as well as do some research about fungus and my findings.
